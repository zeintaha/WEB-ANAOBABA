CultureWays is proud to be involved in ANAOBABA TV, a meaningful and necessary project that aims at reducing the gap between refugees and asylum-seekers and their families back home.

To flygtninge fra Syrien har fået 300.000 kr. til at udvikle et tv-projekt til en selvstændig virksomhed.

Syriske fædre fra Aarhus kommune holder kontakt med deres børn og lærer dem sprog og dansk kultur på deres selvopfundne Anaobaba TV - som på dansk betyder "Mig og far TV". Se indslaget fra TV AVISEN.

Nomineret til Aarhus Takker: Flygtninge optager livet i Aarhus

Vi hjælper flygtningefædre med at være aktive i faderrollen og forberede et nyt liv for deres familier, mens de venter på at blive genforenet.

Anaobaba.tv

ANAOBABA.TV helps refugee fathers to be active in their role as fathers and prepare a new life for their families, while waiting for reunification. The aim is to give hope to others who face the same difficult situation. The name is Arabic and means „Me and Daddy TV“. Using their mobile phones the fathers send small videos with everyday stories to their children. They share what they experience and learn. The fathers have s strong desire to be present and take responsibility in their childrens lives. Through the videos they teach their children about Danish language and culture while adapting to a new place. It strenghtens the bonds within the family and give them something positive to unite around. We will begin by matching the fathers with Danish volunteers. We will help the fathers comprehend laws and rules especially regarding their own family reunification case. That will make them relax. We will participate in activities about language, culture, and the labour market. We will work with practical skills to communicate and use the smartphone. Through all the activities, the fathers will be filming small videos and sending them to their children. ANAOBABA.TV is run as a cooperation between Sager der Samler, the Municipality of Århus and Bikubenfonden. Sager der Samler is in charge of the practical apect led by two project managers who themselves came here as refugee fathers. The Municipality of Århus establishes the contact to the refugee families who are waiting for family reunification and contributes with a part of the funding. Bikubenfonden has provided financial support.
